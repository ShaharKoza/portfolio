My father runs a renovation and project-management business in central
Israel. I built his website: the look, the structure, the copy and the
launch.

Renovation clients browse on their phones, so the site is a single fast
static page with no framework behind it. It's organized around the three
questions a homeowner actually has: what do you do, can I trust you, and
how do I reach you right now.

Going live was treated like a deployment rather than an upload, with a
proper checklist covering the domain, metadata, contact flows and mobile
behavior.
