---
title: 'Start With the Unknowns'
summary: 'A working method for building anything non-trivial: map what you don''t know before writing a line of code, then resolve unknowns in order of risk.'
date: 2026-07-16
draft: true
---

Every project I start now begins the same way: not with code, and not with
a feature list, but with a list of the things I don't yet know.

The idea is simple. Most project failures aren't caused by the work you
knew how to do. They're caused by the unknowns you discovered too late. So
make the unknowns the first deliverable. Write them down, rank them by how
much damage they could do, and resolve the riskiest ones first, before
they're load-bearing.

It converts anxiety into a checklist. It front-loads the failures that are
cheapest to have early. And it makes progress measurable before there's
anything to demo.
